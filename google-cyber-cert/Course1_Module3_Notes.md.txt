## Security Frameworks
- guidelines for building plans to help mitigate risk/threats to data privacy

## Purpose of Security Frameworks
- Protect PII
- Secure financial information
- Identify security weaknesses
- Manage organizational risks
- Align security with business goals

## Components of Security Frameworks
- Identify/document security goals
- Set guidelines to achieve goals
- Implement security processes
- Monitor and communicate results

## Security Designs
- CIA Triad
- NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)
	- voluntary framework consisting of standards, guidelines, best practices to manage risks

## Specific Controls, Frameworks & Compliance
1. The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission - North American Electric Reliability Corporation (FERC-NERC)
	- applies to organizations wanting to work with US/NA power grid
	- obligations to mitigate/report any risks to power grid
2. The Federal Risk & Authorization Management Program (FedRAMP)
	- US Federal govt program
	- security assessments, monitoring, handling of cloud services
	- consistency across govt sectors/3rd-party cloud providers
3. Center for Internet Security (CIS)
	- non-profit providing set of controls to safeguard systems/networks against attacks
	- help organizations establish better plan of defence
4. General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
	- protects EU residents data and right to privacy
	- breach of data must be informed to victim within 72 hours
5. Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCIDSS)
	- international security standard for storing, accepting, processing, transmitting credit card info in secure environment
	- to reduce CC fraud
6. The Health Insurance Portability & Accountability Act (HIPAA)
	- US Federal Law to protect patient health info
	- Governed by 3 rules (Privacy, Security, Breach Notification)
7. International Organization for Standardization (ISO)
	- created for tech, manufacturing, and management across borders
	- improve processes and procedures for staff retention, planning, waste, and services
8. Systems & Organizations Controls (SOC Type 1, SOC Type 2)
	- series of reports used to access organizational financial compliance and levels of risk
	- covers confidentiality, privacy, integrity, availability, security, overall data safety
